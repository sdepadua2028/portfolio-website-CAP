Growth

To me, my recommendation app represents an area of growth because it was one of the first computer science projects my class was assigned. Here, I was able to put my newly learned skills about coding and problem solving to work, which was especially fun while doing a project on a topic that I enjoyed — Greek mythology and the world of Percy Jackson. However, what I found the hardest area to master and one of my obstacles was fairly distributing the points I would give to each possible outcome while taking into consideration all the data I received. I also had to avoid tiebreakers as much as possible, so that the app’s user would not have to change their answers and take the quiz multiple times. So, I gave certain possibilities more points than the others (ex. One option got 5 points while another received 2) based on what I know about each option’s characteristics. I believe that by making sure I did every level in this unit thoroughly and correctly, I was able to make my app as accurate as possible concerning my new coding skills. However, now that I know more about computer science, I would add more options per questions and perhaps more overall possibilities, which would increase the accuracy of my quiz.
